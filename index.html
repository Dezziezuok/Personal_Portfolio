As an Electrical Engineering major with a Computer Science minor, I’m passionate about bridging hardware and software to build intelligent, human-centered systems. My interests lie especially in computer vision and machine learning, where I explore how machines can interpret and respond to the world around them.

Currently, I’m conducting undergraduate research with the Human Technology Interaction Lab at the University of Alabama, focusing on using computer vision and machine learning techniques to recognize human gestures and movements through hardware integration.

I thrive on challenges that push boundaries, and I’m constantly learning, experimenting, and creating to advance the way humans and machines interact.

About Me

I'm an aspiring machine learning engineer, but beyond the screen, at the heart of everything I do is my faith. As a Christian and Nigerian-American, I carry a strong sense of resilience, and purpose, rooted in faith, cultivated by culture, and driven by the desire to make meaningful impacts.

When I’m not buried under books or writing code, you’ll probably find me crocheting something cozy, getting a good workout in at the gym, fine-tuning my budget (yes, I genuinely enjoy it), or scrolling through Pinterest for a spark of inspiration. I believe in being intentional, showing grit, and finding connections and joy in all that I do.

To me, there’s a clear parallel between coding and crocheting loops, both require creativity, patience, and a love for building something meaningful, one stitch or one line of code at a time.
