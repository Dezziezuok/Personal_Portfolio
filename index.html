As an Electrical Engineering major with a minor in Computer Science, I’m passionate about bridging hardware and software to build intelligent, human-centered systems. My interests lie especially in Computer Vision & Natural Language Processing Machine Learning, where I explore how machines can interpret and respond to the world around them.

Currently, I’m conducting undergraduate research with the Human Technology Interaction Lab at the University of Alabama, focusing on using computer vision and machine learning techniques to recognize human gestures and movements through hardware integration.

I thrive on challenges that push boundaries, and I’m constantly learning, experimenting, and creating to advance the way humans and machines interact.

About Me

I’m an aspiring Machine Learning Engineer, but beyond the algorithms and code, my foundation is faith. As a Christian and Nigerian-American, my faith shapes how I create, learn, and connect. It teaches me to approach challenges with purpose, resilience, and curiosity, and to see innovation as a way to serve and inspire others.

My curiosity drives me to explore how technology and art intersect. Whether I’m experimenting with 3D design, building interactive visuals in TouchDesigner, or composing with Strudel, creative coding has become my favorite way to bring ideas to life. Engineering is deeply creative, and every project becomes an opportunity to build something innovative.

To me, engineering isn’t just about solving problems, it’s about creating experiences that resonate. Whether I’m building a ML model, designing generative visuals, or sculpting sound, I’m guided by the same spirit of curiosity and intentionality that drives my faith.

Outside of screens and circuits, I find creativity in other forms such as crotcheting cozy patterns, spending time with friends and family, or even fine-tuning my budget finance tracker in Notion.

Each thread of my life is connected by a desire to grow, create with intention, and stay true to myself.
