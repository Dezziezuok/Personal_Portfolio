Community-Driven Initiatives


UA TOM

[Tikkun Olam Makers The University of Alabama Chapter]

Tikkun Olam Makers, University of Alabama Chapter, is a student organization dedicated to engineering low-cost, open-source assistive devices, has garnered global recognition for its innovative approach to accessible technology.

Rise Project
We are currently collaborating with educators at a the Rise Center, Tuscaloosa to design an interactive educational game for children with varying abilities. I’m using SolidWorks to create 3D-printable components that make learning more engaging and hands-on for students.

Fall Detection Device
Currently collaborating with a multidisciplinary team to design and develop a wearable fall detection device for older adults. The prototype will use an Arduino with built-in motion sensors to detect sudden impacts and abnormal movement patterns. I’m working on integrating the Arduino with additional hardware components and contributing to the ongoing embedded system design and testing.

UA GWC

[Girls Who Code The University of Alabama Chapter]

Girls Who Code, University of Alabama Chapter, is a student organization dedicated to closing the gender gap in technology by empowering girls and non-binary students with the skills and confidence to enter the computer science field. Our Chapter specifically focuses on the youth outreach programs hosted for local middle and high school students.

Girls Who Code Workshop
We hosted an HTML/CSS workshop for middle and high school students at the Tuscaloosa Library, where participants learned how to build their own personal websites. We provided laptops for students who needed them, guided them with step-by-step educational slides, and offered one-on-one assistance to ensure everyone could follow along and successfully create their personal portfolios in Replit. We managed to make it on the Official Girls Who Code Newsletter.

Girls Who Hackathon
We hosted a youth hackathon where over 20 middle and high school students came together to design their own websites. We kicked off the event with overview slides, then let participants have free range of the type of site they wanted to create. Laptops, one-on-one guidance, food, and drinks were provided to support everyone throughout the afternoon. The event ended with many exciting websites and prizes for the top projects, with the winning team creating a Bob Ross dedication website.
